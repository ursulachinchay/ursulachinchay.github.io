How to look after Oily skin

How do you know if you have Oily skin?

Oily skin is one of the most common skin types and is completely normal to have. Common symptoms of oily skin can be an shiny complexion and having often acne breakouts. Oily skin occurs when your skin produces too much sebum, which is a natural oil substance our skin naturally produces. Producing too much of this oil can often lead to shineyness and breakouts. A skincare routine using the correct products can stabalize symptoms from becoming worse This page will help you create the perfect skincare routine with the best products for Oily skin at a budget friendly cost..

1. Using a cleanser for Oily skin

In every skincare routine no matter what skin type you have, using a cleanser should always be used as the first step in your routine. The cleansers above have been tested by dermatologists to be some of the best cleansers for oily skin. When choosing a cleanser for oily skin it's best to pick one that contains either salicylic acid, glycolic acid or lactic acid. These acids helps reduce the excess amount of oil people with oily skin produce and reduces the look of shinyness during the day. Cleansers aimed to treat oily skin can also reduce the look of pores, blackheads and breakouts your skin produces due to excess amount of oil in the skin. A good cleanser will do wonders for oily skin as it recudes the amount of oil produced in your skin during the day. When you have oily skin you must use a cleanser day and night as it removes buildup of oils from the night and during the day.

2. Using a Toner for Oily Skin

After using a cleanser, your skin will feel clean and will be time to use a toner. Like the cleanser, if you struggle with oily skin it's best to use a toner with ingredients such as salicylic acid, glycolic acid and lactic acid. The products shown above contain these ingredients and are great for oily skin. The toner removes any last trces of dirt and skin leftover from cleansing, this way the toner will help prevent clogged pores and minimize the look of blackheads. The toner also removes the look of breakouts, controls oil production, blackheads and prevents your pores from being clogged. People will oily skin are more prone to breakouts and acne, as toners prevent clogged pores it will prevent breakouts from occuring more often. It is great to use a toner after a cleanser as they both work together to impact your skin positively from the excessive producement of oils.

2. Using Serums for Oily skin

For oily skin, when it comes to searching for the suitable serum to match your skin it is important to pick one with the right ingredients. Serums made for oily skin should include ingredients such as Niacinamide, Salycylic acid and Vitamin C. The serums in the image above are tested by dermatologist to be one of the best serums for oily skin. Niacinamide helps regulate the making of sebum which is excess oil your skin produces. It can also help with minimizing the appearance of pores and help with breakouts as when having oily skin, your skin is known to be acne prone. Salycylic acid is known to maintain pores and unclog them as well as reducing blackheads which is a common symptom of oily skin. Vitamin C is great at maintaining the excess amount of oil your skin produces when having oily skin and protects the skin of harsh environments as when dealing with this skin type, your skin tends to be very fragile and sensitive.

3. Using a Moisturizer for Oily Skin

When it comes to using a moisturizer with oily skin, it is recommended to choose the lightweight option. A lightweight moisturizer is best used for oily skin as they are less likely to clog pores. When your pores are clogged, it is more likely for breakouts to occur when pores are blocked. A normal moisturizer could create a thick layer on your skin which could trap the oil produced from your skin and cause more skin concerns. When you use a lightweight moisturizer it is absorbed into the skin faster and will not leave a shiny cast on your skin. A lightweight moisturizer will give the skin just the right amount of hydration it needs without adding unnecessary oils into the skin whereas, normal moisturizers will make the skin produce even more oiliness your skin does not need. The moisturizers shown above are all great lightweight options of moisturizers for oily skin.

4. Using Sunscreen for Oily Skin

No matter what skin type you have, it is always important to include a sunscreen in your routine as it protects your skin from the harmful effect of the sun. The UV rays from the sun can cause skin concerns such as sunburn, premature aging, and the risk of skin cancer. Sunscreen will protect you of these harmful effects of the sun and should be used no matter what skin type you are. The sunscreens in the image above are specifically made for oily skin as they are "non-comedogenic". This means that sunscreens with this ingredient will not clog pores, this is great for acne-prone skin such as oily skin. These sunscreens above are also fragrance free, products with fragrance irritates the skin and can cause skin reactions such as breakouts. Non-fragranced sunscreens are better as they will not irritate the skin because oily skin tends to be sensitive. Lightweight sunscreens should also be used for oily skin as it absorbs fast into the skin and will not give a greasy residue in the skin making it more greasy than it already is. Ensure to always use a sunscreen everyday only in the mornings, to provide you protection from the sun for the day.
